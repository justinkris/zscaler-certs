STUDY NIBBLE

Risk360 vs Breach Predictor

Two risk-telemetry capabilities, two very different questions.

ZIA Risk Management
The one-liner
Risk360 = where you are. Breach Predictor = where you're heading.
Dimension Risk360 Breach Predictor
Question it answers "How risky am I right now?" "What attack is most likely to happen next, and how would it unfold?"
Output A score + four risk factors + remediation recommendations A predicted attack scenario / kill-chain narrative
Time horizon Current state (snapshot) Forward-looking (forecast)
Nature Diagnostic Prognostic
Method Aggregates posture, config, and telemetry into a single score ML models pattern-match your telemetry against known attack progressions
Scoring direction High = more risk, Low = less risk (fever thermometer) Likelihood-based — ranks scenarios by probability
Primary audience CISO / board (risk posture reporting) SOC / security ops (proactive threat hunting)
⟳
WEATHER ANALOGY
Risk360 is the current reading — "It's 28°C with 70% humidity right now."
Breach Predictor is the forecast — "70% chance of thunderstorms on Thursday."
Same sensors, different question.
→
EXAM TIP
If a question mentions scoring, posture, or "current risk level" → Risk360. If it mentions prediction, forecast, likely attack path, or "what could happen next" → Breach Predictor. Both use similar underlying data, so distractor answers will feel plausible — anchor on the verb tense of the question.
