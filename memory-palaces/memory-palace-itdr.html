EXAM TIPS

When ITDR is the answer

→
EXAM TIP
If the scenario mentions privilege escalation, compromised AD accounts, or suspicious identity behaviour — ITDR is almost always the answer, not ZIA ATP or Sandbox.
→
EXAM TIP
Questions about correlating risk signals across ZIA, ZPA, and Okta into a single view point directly to Identity Risk Profile — ITDR's cross-product signal correlation pillar.
★
MENTAL NOTE
ITDR vs Deception: Deception = attacker touches the tripwire. ITDR = attacker's behaviour shifts the risk signal. One is a trap; the other is a monitor.
PILLAR GUIDE

What each pillar does

ITDR (Identity Threat Detection & Response) covers six capabilities — each one a different layer of identity defence. Know these cold before exam day.

01
Continuous Identity Posture Assessment
Non-stop vulnerability scanning of Active Directory and Entra ID. Constantly checks for misconfigurations, weak permissions, and risky account states — not a one-time audit, but an always-on monitor.
02
Change Detection
Configurable watchlists that alert on identity changes 24/7. If a user's group membership, admin rights, or login behaviour shifts unexpectedly, ITDR flags it immediately rather than waiting for a scheduled review.
03
Attack Detection & Alerts
Detects active AD attack techniques in real time: Kerberoasting (cracking service account hashes offline), DCSync (attacker impersonates a DC to pull password hashes), and DCShadow (injecting malicious changes through AD replication).
04
Endpoint Credential Exposure Management
Scans endpoints for credentials stored insecurely — in plain text files, browser caches, or environment variables. Flagging and removing these cuts off the lateral movement paths attackers rely on after initial access.
05
Identity Risk Profile
Correlates signals from ZIA, ZPA, and Okta into a single risk score per identity. Unusual web traffic in ZIA + anomalous app access in ZPA + suspicious login in Okta = elevated risk score for that user, visible in one place.
06
Response & Adaptive Access Control
When risk spikes, ZIA and ZPA isolate the identity from the network while Okta AI enforces step-up authentication (MFA re-challenge). The user is contained and forced to re-verify — without a full lockout that could disrupt legitimate work.

QUICK REFERENCE
KERBEROASTING
Exploits weak service account passwords in AD to extract hashed credentials offline.
DCSYNC
Attacker impersonates a domain controller to pull password hashes from AD replication.
DCSHADOW
Attacker injects malicious changes into AD by manipulating the replication process itself.
STEP-UP AUTH
Adaptive response that demands re-verification (e.g. MFA challenge) when risk score spikes.
